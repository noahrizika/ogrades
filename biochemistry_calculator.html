    DNA Stacking Energy Calculator

    DNA is stabilized by the hydrogen bonds between paired nucelotides on corresponding strands. However, the interactions between stacked nucelotides on each strand play an even larger role in stabilizing DNA. This program calculates the stacking energies of a double-stranded DNA (dsDNA). Lower energies indicate greater stability.

    RNA Coding Strand

    RNA consists of the same nucelotides as DNA, except Uracil is used instead of Adenine. One section of a DNA coding strand, read from the 5' end to the 3' end, encodes for a specific RNA sequence. This RNA sequence contains the instructions to form proteins that the body needs. This program returns the RNA version of a coding DNA strand.
